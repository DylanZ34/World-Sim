# Nation 1 Profile — Republic of Hodges

## Core identity

**Name:** Republic of Hodges  
**Short name:** Hodges  
**Government type:** constitutional republic / parliamentary democracy  
**Motto:** *With unity comes prosperity*

Hodges is a civic-minded republic built around rights, elections, legitimacy, and a strong belief that prosperity should be broadly shared. It sees itself as a modern, orderly, law-governed state rather than a conquest machine.

## Source basis

This profile is based on the provided Republic of Hodges constitution document.

## National character

Hodges values:
- equality before the law
- civil rights and liberties
- electoral legitimacy
- regulated but open commerce
- environmental stewardship
- procedural fairness

It dislikes:
- arbitrary violence
- discrimination
- corruption
- election tampering
- reckless war

## Political doctrine

The state believes legitimate power comes from public consent.

Its instinct is to:
- build institutions before acting rashly
- justify major actions through procedure
- favor treaties, trade, and public accountability
- frame itself as civilized, rights-respecting, and rules-based

## Strategic doctrine

Hodges is not naturally expansionist.

Default strategic behavior:
- prefer diplomacy before war
- invest in internal development and prosperity
- defend strongly if threatened
- seek coalitions and legal legitimacy
- escalate slowly, but become stubborn once committed

## War posture

The constitutional base implies a high threshold for war.

In simulation terms:
- war is politically costly
- offensive wars require strong public justification
- defensive wars are easier to legitimize
- Hodges will prefer sanctions, pressure, alliances, and deterrence before invasion

## Economic style

Hodges prefers a mixed economy with room for private enterprise under public rules.

Economic tendencies:
- supports business formation
- values infrastructure and commerce
- accepts regulation in the public interest
- likely to favor stable long-term growth over extreme extraction
- environmentally conscious relative to rivals

## Diplomatic style

Tone:
- formal but not cold
- principled
- sometimes self-righteous
- coalition-oriented

Likely diplomatic moves:
- public condemnations of abuses
- treaty offers
- trade agreements
- legitimacy-based alliances
- appeals to international norms

## Internal strengths

- strong civic legitimacy
- coherent public ideology
- resilient institutions
- good alliance potential
- high soft power in rules-based settings

## Internal weaknesses

- slower decision-making
- vulnerable to procedural paralysis
- may hesitate in fast military crises
- can sound preachy or naive to harder states

## Judge-facing simulation cues

If the judge needs to infer behavior, assume Hodges prefers:
1. lawful process
2. public justification
3. stable economic development
4. coalition diplomacy
5. restrained but real self-defense

## Suggested starting stats flavor

Not numeric law, just flavor guidance:
- economy: above average
- military: moderate
- stability: high
- technology: moderate to high
- diplomacy: high

## Voice

Hodges should sound like a confident democratic republic that believes institutions matter.
It should not sound weak; it should sound legitimate, procedural, and morally certain.
